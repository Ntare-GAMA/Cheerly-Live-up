Quick Coping Techniques

❓ 4-7-8 Breathing Technique

Great for reducing anxiety and promoting calmness

  • Breathe in quietly through your nose for 4 counts
  • Hold your breath for 7 counts
  • Exhale completely through your mouth for 8 counts
  • Repeat this cycle 3-4 times

❓ 5-4-3-2-1 Grounding Exercise

Use your senses to ground yourself in the present moment

  • Name 5 things you can see around you
  • Name 4 things you can touch or feel
  • Name 3 things you can hear
  • Name 2 things you can smell
  • Name 1 thing you can taste

❓ Progressive Muscle Relaxation

Release physical tension and promote relaxation

  • Start with your toes, tense the muscles for 5 seconds
  • Release and notice the feeling of relaxation
  • Move up through your body: legs, abdomen, arms, shoulders, face
  • Breathe slowly and deeply throughout the exercise

❓ Digital Detox Practice

Reduce screen time for better mental clarity

  • Set specific times to check social media (e.g., only 3 times per day)
  • Turn off non-essential notifications
  • Create phone-free zones (bedroom, dinner table)
  • Replace scrolling time with activities you enjoy

Understanding Mental Health

❓

What is Mental Health?

Mental health includes our emotional, psychological, and social wellbeing. It affects how we think, feel, and act. Understanding mental health is the first step to taking care of it.

💚

Breaking the Stigma

Mental health challenges are common and nothing to be ashamed of. Talking openly about mental health helps reduce stigma and encourages others to seek support when needed.

❓

Signs You Might Need Support

Persistent sadness, anxiety, changes in sleep or appetite, difficulty concentrating, withdrawal from activities, or thoughts of self-harm are all signs it's time to reach out for help.

❓

Self-Care is Not Selfish

Taking time to care for your mental health is essential, not selfish. Regular self-care practices help prevent burnout and build resilience for life's challenges.

❓

When and How to Seek Professional Help

If mental health challenges interfere with daily life, relationships, or work/school, it's time to seek professional support. Start with a trusted adult, school counselor, or healthcare provider.
